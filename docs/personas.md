# User Personas

Personas turn abstract "users" into concrete people with goals, frustrations, and daily realities. They keep design and product decisions grounded. Each persona below describes **goals, pain points, daily workflow, motivation, and success criteria**.

Personas are grouped as **End Users** (people managing their own money) and **Professional Users** (people who advise others).

---

## End Users

### 1. The Student — "Aanya, 20"
*First income, learning the ropes.*

- **Goals:** Stop running out of money before month-end; start a small savings habit; understand where money goes.
- **Pain points:** Irregular income (part-time work, allowance); no financial education; intimidated by finance jargon; tight budget.
- **Daily workflow:** Checks balance on phone; makes small frequent purchases; occasionally panics near month-end.
- **Motivation:** Independence; not asking parents for money; feeling "adult."
- **Success criteria:** Has a small but growing savings buffer; understands her spending; feels less anxious about money.
- **How WealthWise helps:** Simple expense tracking, gentle nudges, bite-sized education, free AI explanations.

### 2. The Young Professional — "Marco, 27"
*Earning real money, wants to build wealth.*

- **Goals:** Build an emergency fund; start investing; save for travel and a future home; pay off student debt.
- **Pain points:** Lifestyle creep; doesn't know how to start investing; analysis paralysis; scattered tools.
- **Daily workflow:** Salaried, predictable income; uses several apps; researches investing sporadically.
- **Motivation:** Get ahead early; FOMO about investing; build security.
- **Success criteria:** Funded emergency fund, automated savings, first investments made with understanding.
- **How WealthWise helps:** Goal planning, AI Coach for "where do I start," eventually investment planning and an advisor when stakes rise.

### 3. The Married Family — "Priya & Sam, 34"
*Two incomes, shared goals, growing complexity.*

- **Goals:** Coordinate household budget; save for a home and children's education; align on financial priorities.
- **Pain points:** Two people, two money styles; hard to see the full household picture; big decisions feel risky.
- **Daily workflow:** Juggling shared and personal expenses; periodic "money talks" that can be tense.
- **Motivation:** Security and a good life for the family; reducing money-related stress in the relationship.
- **Success criteria:** A shared, agreed plan; visible progress on big goals; fewer money conflicts.
- **How WealthWise helps:** Household/goal coordination (future family accounts), reports both partners trust, advisor for big decisions.

### 4. The Parent — "David, 41"
*Providing and planning for the next generation.*

- **Goals:** Education savings; adequate life insurance; balance present needs with future security.
- **Pain points:** Many competing financial priorities; limited time; worry about doing right by the kids.
- **Daily workflow:** Busy; wants periodic check-ins, not daily fiddling; values automation and reminders.
- **Motivation:** Protecting and providing for family.
- **Success criteria:** Insurance and education plans in place; on track for retirement without sacrificing the present.
- **How WealthWise helps:** Insurance and education planning, proactive nudges, advisor for protection planning.

### 5. The Business Owner — "Lena, 45"
*Personal and business finances intertwined.*

- **Goals:** Separate personal from business money; manage irregular income; plan taxes; build personal wealth alongside the business.
- **Pain points:** Cash-flow volatility; tax complexity; commingled finances; little time.
- **Daily workflow:** Variable income; thinks in terms of the business but neglects personal planning.
- **Motivation:** Build personal security independent of the business; reduce tax surprises.
- **Success criteria:** Clear separation, tax readiness, personal wealth growing independently of the business.
- **How WealthWise helps:** Tax planning, cash-flow-aware budgeting, advisor/agency for complex situations.

### 6. The Retired User — "Robert, 67"
*Preserving and drawing down wealth.*

- **Goals:** Make savings last; manage withdrawals; minimize tax; leave a legacy.
- **Pain points:** Fear of outliving money; market anxiety; complexity of drawdown and tax; sometimes lower tech comfort.
- **Daily workflow:** Periodic review; values clarity and reassurance over features.
- **Motivation:** Security, dignity, and legacy.
- **Success criteria:** Confidence that the plan is sustainable; clear, calm guidance.
- **How WealthWise helps:** Retirement drawdown views, plain explanations, easy access to a trusted human advisor; accessibility-first design.

## Professional Users

### 7. The Independent Financial Advisor — "Carlos, 38"
*A solo professional building a practice.*

- **Goals:** Find well-matched clients; serve them well with less admin; build a strong reputation; grow income.
- **Pain points:** Client acquisition is hard and costly; administrative overhead eats billable time; hard to demonstrate trustworthiness to strangers.
- **Daily workflow:** Meetings, follow-ups, plan preparation, prospecting. Wants the platform to handle scheduling, context, and busywork.
- **Motivation:** Independence and a thriving practice; genuinely helping clients.
- **Success criteria:** A full, well-matched client roster; strong ratings; more time spent advising, less on admin.
- **How WealthWise helps:** Marketplace exposure, client context already in place, AI to draft and summarize, scheduling and messaging tools.

### 8. The Agency Owner — "Fatima, 50"
*Runs a wealth-management firm with multiple advisors.*

- **Goals:** Manage a team and a book of clients; ensure quality and compliance; grow the firm; maintain the brand.
- **Pain points:** Coordinating advisors; ensuring consistent service; oversight without micromanaging; data security across many clients.
- **Daily workflow:** Oversees advisors, monitors performance, handles escalations, manages business development.
- **Motivation:** Build a respected, profitable firm.
- **Success criteria:** High advisor utilization, strong client retention, clean compliance, growing book of business.
- **How WealthWise helps:** Agency Portal for team and client management, strict data isolation, oversight dashboards, branding, business model support.

### 9. The Financial Planner — "Grace, 33"
*An advisor within an agency, focused on planning depth.*

- **Goals:** Deliver high-quality plans efficiently; manage an assigned client load; advance her career.
- **Pain points:** Repetitive plan preparation; context-switching across clients; demonstrating value.
- **Daily workflow:** Builds and reviews financial plans, meets clients, collaborates within the agency.
- **Motivation:** Mastery of the craft; client outcomes; professional growth.
- **Success criteria:** Efficient, high-quality plans; satisfied clients; recognized contribution within the agency.
- **How WealthWise helps:** Planning tools, AI-assisted drafting and summarization, shared client context within the agency.

### 10. The Corporate / Enterprise Client — "Nordic Manufacturing, HR Lead"
*An organization offering financial wellness to its people.*

- **Goals:** Improve employees' financial wellbeing; reduce financial stress that affects productivity; offer a valued benefit.
- **Pain points:** Generic wellness programs with low engagement; privacy concerns; measuring impact.
- **Daily workflow:** HR/benefits administration; periodic reporting to leadership.
- **Motivation:** A healthier, more engaged workforce; a differentiated benefits offering.
- **Success criteria:** High employee adoption, measurable wellbeing improvement, clean privacy posture.
- **How WealthWise helps:** Enterprise/white-label offering, aggregate (privacy-preserving) reporting, scalable access to tools and education.

## Using personas

- **Designers** map journeys and interfaces against the personas most affected (see [Designers guide](knowledge-transfer/designers.md)).
- **Product** prioritizes the backlog by persona impact (see [Roadmap](roadmap.md)).
- **AI Engineers** calibrate tone and depth of guidance per persona (a student needs encouragement and basics; a retiree needs reassurance and clarity).

Personas are hypotheses to be validated with real user research as WealthWise grows; treat them as living, not fixed.
