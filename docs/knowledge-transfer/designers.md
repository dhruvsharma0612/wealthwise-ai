# Knowledge Transfer: Designers

> For UX/UI designers shaping the WealthWise experience.

## What is WealthWise?
An AI-powered financial ecosystem where users choose how much help they want — managing money themselves, with AI, or with human advisors and agencies. Your job is to make something inherently stressful (money) feel **calm, clear, and trustworthy**. See [Platform Overview](../platform-overview.md).

## Why does it exist?
Because finance is intimidating, fragmented, and opaque. Design is central to the fix: clarity and trust are *experiential* qualities you create. See [Vision](../vision.md).

## How do you use it (as a designer)?
- **Design for the personas**, not a generic user. A [student](../personas.md) needs encouragement and simplicity; a [retiree](../personas.md) needs reassurance and accessibility; an [advisor](../personas.md) needs efficiency. See [Personas](../personas.md).
- **Embody the principles in the UI:**
  - **Transparency** → make the "why?" always reachable; never a black box ([BR-03](../business-rules.md#br-03--every-recommendation-must-be-explainable)).
  - **Trust** → honest defaults, no dark patterns, clear pricing and data use.
  - **Accessibility** → inclusive, plain-language, progressive disclosure so power doesn't overwhelm.
  - **User First & control** → the user always feels in control; nothing happens by surprise ([BR-18](../business-rules.md)).
- **Design the independence spectrum** — make moving between self-directed, AI, and human help feel seamless, judgment-free, and continuous (context carries over).

## What are your responsibilities?
- Reduce anxiety: calm, legible, non-judgmental interfaces around money.
- Make AI feel **helpful and honest** — clearly AI (never pretending to be human), always explainable, never pushy.
- Make trust **visible**: verification, ratings, data ownership, and access controls should be understandable in the UI ([Advisor Ecosystem](../advisor-ecosystem.md)).
- Practice **progressive disclosure**: serve the beginner and the power user in one product.

## What value do you receive?
- Rich personas and journeys to design against.
- Clear principles that resolve design trade-offs (e.g., simplicity vs. depth → accessibility wins via progressive disclosure).
- A product where good design directly creates the core value: understanding and trust.

## Design north stars
- Could a 20-year-old and a 67-year-old both use this comfortably?
- Can the user always find out **why** something is recommended?
- Does the user feel **in control** at every step?
- Does the experience build **trust** or quietly spend it?
