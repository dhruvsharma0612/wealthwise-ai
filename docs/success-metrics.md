# Success Metrics

Metrics tell us whether WealthWise is achieving its [Mission](mission.md) and moving toward its [Vision](vision.md). They keep the team honest: a feature that doesn't move a metric that matters isn't really working.

Everything ladders up to the **North Star**.

---

## North Star

> **Did the user end the period financially better off — and did they understand why?**

Engagement, revenue, and growth matter, but they are *downstream* of users genuinely improving their financial lives *with understanding*. We are wary of metrics that reward usage without benefit.

A practical North Star metric: **the share of active users showing measurable financial improvement** (e.g., improved savings rate, reduced high-interest debt, or goal progress) over a rolling period.

---

## Metric categories

### 1. User Growth
- New user sign-ups
- Activation rate (users who complete onboarding and capture their first financial picture)
- Organic vs. referred growth
- Conversion from free to premium

*Why:* Reach is the precondition for impact — but growth without retention or benefit is hollow.

### 2. Retention & Engagement
- Retention (e.g., monthly active / returning users over time)
- Habit formation (regular check-ins, recurring use of core tools)
- Engagement with **explanations and education** (a proxy for *understanding*, not just usage)

*Why:* Sustained value shows up as people coming back; engagement with understanding is the kind we want.

### 3. Financial Outcomes (the heart of the North Star)
- **Savings improvement** — change in users' savings rate over time
- **Goal completion** — goals reached / on track
- **Debt reduction** — reduction in high-interest debt among active users
- **Emergency fund coverage** — share of users with an adequate buffer
- **Net worth trend** — directional improvement for engaged users

*Why:* This is what "better off" actually means. These are the metrics we most want to move.

### 4. AI Engagement & Quality
- AI Coach usage and repeat use
- Helpfulness/satisfaction of AI interactions
- Explanation comprehension (do users act on or understand AI explanations?)
- Safe-behavior adherence (AI staying within [business rules](business-rules.md)) — a quality/guardrail metric

*Why:* AI is central; we measure both adoption and whether it's genuinely, safely helpful.

### 5. Advisor & Agency Health
- **Advisor activity** — active advisors, client load, response times
- **Agency growth** — agencies onboarded, advisors per agency, client books
- Client–advisor match quality and retention
- Advisor/agency satisfaction

*Why:* A thriving professional ecosystem sustains the human-help side of the value proposition.

### 6. Customer Satisfaction & Trust
- Customer satisfaction (e.g., NPS or equivalent)
- Trust indicators (perceived transparency, privacy confidence)
- Support resolution time and quality
- Complaint and escalation rates

*Why:* Trust is the product ([Principles](principles.md)); we must measure it, not just assume it.

### 7. Revenue & Sustainability
- Subscription revenue (individual, premium, advisor, agency, enterprise)
- Revenue retention / churn
- Unit economics health
- Ecosystem revenue (advisor/agency activity)

*Why:* A sustainable business is what lets the mission endure — but never pursued at the cost of trust or privacy.

## Metrics by roadmap phase

Each [phase](roadmap.md) emphasizes different metrics:

| Phase | Primary metrics to watch |
|---|---|
| Phase 1 (Personal Finance MVP) | Activation, retention, savings/goal progress, AI explanation engagement |
| Phase 2 (Human Help & AI Coach) | Advisor/agency activity, match quality, AI Coach usage, satisfaction |

## Guardrail metrics (must not get worse)

Some metrics exist to ensure we don't win one number by harming another:

- **Trust/privacy confidence** must not decline as we grow or monetize.
- **AI safety adherence** (staying within business rules) must remain effectively total.
- **Data-isolation integrity** — zero tolerance for cross-agency or unauthorized access.
- **User wellbeing** — engagement should correlate with benefit, not anxiety or dark-pattern stickiness.

---

## Principles for using metrics

1. **Outcomes over vanity.** Prefer financial-improvement metrics over raw usage.
2. **Understanding counts.** Measure comprehension, not just clicks.
3. **Guardrails are non-negotiable.** A gain that breaches a guardrail is not a gain.
4. **Privacy-preserving measurement.** We measure in ways that respect [BR-10](business-rules.md#br-10--privacy-is-mandatory-not-optional) — aggregate and consented, never invasive.
5. **Honest reporting.** Metrics are reported faithfully, including when they're bad.

> Targets and exact definitions are set at the start of each phase against the relevant personas and goals; this document defines *what* we measure and *why*.
